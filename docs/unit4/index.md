# Unit 4: Revolutions in Modern Physics — Lesson Planner

!!! info "Unit overview"
    **Notional time:** 55 hours (including assessment)  
    Students examine observations that could not be explained by classical physics, leading to special relativity, quantum theory, and the Standard Model of particle physics.

## Topic 1: Special relativity (16 hours)

### Module 9 — Time and motion

| Lesson | Time | Focus | Subject matter |
|--------|------|-------|---------------|
| 9.1 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Einstein's postulates of special relativity. Frames of reference. Inertial and non-inertial frames. |
| 9.2 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | [Time dilation](special-relativity.md#time-dilation): \(t = \frac{t_0}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}}\). Proper time vs relativistic time. |
| 9.3 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Solve time dilation problems. Experimental evidence (muon decay). |

### Module 10 — Length, momentum and energy

| Lesson | Time | Focus | Subject matter |
|--------|------|-------|---------------|
| 10.1 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | [Length contraction](special-relativity.md#length-contraction): \(l = l_0\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}\). Proper length. |
| 10.2 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | [Relativistic momentum](special-relativity.md#relativistic-momentum): \(p = \frac{mv}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}}\). |
| 10.3 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | [Mass–energy equivalence](special-relativity.md#mass-energy-equivalence): \(E = mc^2\). Why no object with mass can travel at \(c\). |
| 10.4 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Paradoxes: twins' paradox, ladder-in-the-barn. |
| 10.5 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Problem-solving and consolidation. |
| 10.6 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | **Review:** Special relativity. |

## Topic 2: Quantum theory (16 hours)

### Module 11 — Quantum theory and light

| Lesson | Time | Focus | Subject matter |
|--------|------|-------|---------------|
| 11.1 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | [Black-body radiation](quantum-theory.md#black-body-radiation). Wien's displacement law. Ultraviolet catastrophe. |
| 11.2 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Planck's hypothesis. Quantisation of energy: \(E = hf\). |
| 11.3 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | [Photoelectric effect](quantum-theory.md#photoelectric-effect): \(E_k = hf - \phi\). Threshold frequency. |
| 11.4 | 60 min | <span class="badge-practical">Practical</span> | **Practical: Photoelectric effect simulation** (PhET or equivalent). |
| 11.5 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Wave–particle duality of light. Evidence for photon model. |

### Module 12 — Quantum theory and atoms

| Lesson | Time | Focus | Subject matter |
|--------|------|-------|---------------|
| 12.1 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | [Bohr model](quantum-theory.md#bohr-model) of the atom. Electron energy levels. |
| 12.2 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Emission and absorption spectra. \(E = hf = \frac{hc}{\lambda}\). |
| 12.3 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | de Broglie wavelength: \(\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}\). Wave–particle duality of matter. |
| 12.4 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Heisenberg uncertainty principle. |
| 12.5 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | **Review:** Quantum theory. |
