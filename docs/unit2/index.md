# Unit 2: Linear Motion & Waves — Lesson Planner

!!! info "Unit overview"
    **Notional time:** 55 hours (including assessment)  
    Students describe linear motion, examine relationships between force, momentum and energy, and investigate wave phenomena including sound and light.

## Topic 1: Linear motion and force (25 hours)

### Module 9 — Linear motion

| Lesson | Time | Focus | Subject matter |
|--------|------|-------|---------------|
| 9.1 | 30 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Vectors and scalars. Symbolise vectors graphically and algebraically. |
| 9.2 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Distance and displacement. Resultant vectors in one dimension. |
| 9.3 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Speed and velocity. Compare instantaneous and average velocity. |
| 9.4 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Graphs of linear motion — constant velocity. Interpret s–t and v–t graphs. |
| 9.5 | 90 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Uniformly accelerated motion graphs. Areas under v–t and a–t graphs. |
| 9.6 | 60 min | <span class="badge-practical">Practical</span> | **Practical: s–t and v–t graphs for a ball on an incline.** |
| 9.7 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | [SUVAT equations](linear-motion.md#equations-of-motion). Solve kinematic problems. |
| 9.8 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Acceleration due to gravity. Motion under gravity. |
| 9.9 | 60 min | <span class="badge-practical">Practical</span> | **Practical: Acceleration due to gravity** — linearise \(t^2\) vs \(s\) data. |

### Module 10 — Classical mechanics

| Lesson | Time | Focus | Subject matter |
|--------|------|-------|---------------|
| 10.1 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Forces and [free-body diagrams](linear-motion.md#newtons-laws). Resultant force in 1D. |
| 10.2 | 30 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Newton's three laws. SHE — Newton, Émilie du Châtelet. |
| 10.3–10.4 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Solve problems: \(a = \frac{F_{net}}{m}\). |
| 10.5 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | [Momentum and impulse](linear-motion.md#momentum-and-impulse). Conservation of momentum. |
| 10.6 | 60 min | <span class="badge-practical">Practical</span> | **Practical: Conservation of momentum in collisions.** |
| 10.7 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Collision problems: \(\sum mv_{before} = \sum mv_{after}\). |

### Module 11 — Energy

| Lesson | Time | Focus | Subject matter |
|--------|------|-------|---------------|
| 11.1 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Work, kinetic energy, gravitational potential energy. [Energy problems](linear-motion.md#energy). |
| 11.2 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Area under force–displacement graph. Energy–time graphs. |
| 11.3–11.4 | 120 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Elastic vs inelastic collisions. Solve problems. |
