# Unit 1: Thermal, Nuclear & Electrical Physics — Lesson Planner

!!! info "Unit overview"
    **Notional time:** 56 hours (including assessment)  
    Students explore energy transfers and transformations pivotal to modern industrial societies — heating processes, nuclear reactions, and electricity.

## Topic 1: Heating processes (15 hours)

### Module 2 — Kinetic particle model and specific heat capacity

| Lesson | Time | Focus | Subject matter |
|--------|------|-------|---------------|
| 2.1 | 30 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Introduce the difference between heat and energy. Describe thermal energy, temperature, kinetic energy, heat and internal energy. |
| 2.2 | 60 min | <span class="badge-practical">Practical</span> | **Practical: Heating water on a hotplate.** Investigate the proportional relationship between heat and temperature change. Graphing and analysing data. |
| 2.3 | 30 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Describe the kinetic particle model of matter. |
| 2.4 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Describe the kinetic particle model of matter. Describe microscopic, macroscopic, internal and thermal energy. Total energy of a system. |
| 2.5 | 30 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Kinetic energy distribution and how heating affects kinetic energy and temperature. Explain that a change in temperature is due to the addition or removal of energy from a system (without phase change). |
| 2.6 | 30 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Measuring temperature and temperature scales. Use \(T_K = T_C + 273\) to convert temperature measurements. |
| 2.7 | 30 min | <span class="badge-practical">Practical</span> | **Practical: Precision and accuracy of thermometers.** Investigate the precision and accuracy of different temperature measuring devices by determining measurement uncertainty. |
| 2.8 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Explain heat transfers in terms of conduction, convection and radiation. |
| 2.9 | 30 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Describe the concept of [specific heat capacity](heating-processes.md#specific-heat-capacity). Solve problems using \(Q = mc\Delta T\). |
| 2.10 | 60 min | <span class="badge-practical">Practical</span> | **Practical: Specific heat capacity of a metal — on a hotplate.** Investigate specific heat capacity of a substance. |
| 2.11 | 30 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | **Review:** Kinetic particle model and specific heat capacity — multiple choice, short response and data analysis questions. |

### Module 3 — Phase changes and energy conservation

| Lesson | Time | Focus | Subject matter |
|--------|------|-------|---------------|
| 3.1 | 90 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Explain why the temperature of a system remains the same during phase change (internal energy and kinetic particle model). Describe [specific latent heat](heating-processes.md#specific-latent-heat). Solve problems using \(Q = mL\). |
| 3.2 | 30 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Describe [thermal equilibrium](heating-processes.md#thermal-equilibrium) in terms of temperature and average kinetic energy. Recognise the zeroth law of thermodynamics. |
| 3.3 | 120 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Solve problems involving specific heat capacity, specific latent heat and thermal equilibrium. |
| 3.4 | 60 min | <span class="badge-practical">Practical</span> | **Practical: Specific heat capacity of liquids — by calorimetry.** Use digital measuring devices, correct SI units, significant figures and measurement uncertainty. |
| 3.5 | 60 min | <span class="badge-practical">Practical</span> | **Practical: Mixture of two liquids — calorimetry.** Investigate percentage error by comparing theoretical and measured temperatures. |
| 3.6 | 30 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Explain how a system with thermal energy has the capacity to do mechanical work. |
| 3.7 | 30 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | [First law of thermodynamics](heating-processes.md#first-law-of-thermodynamics): \(\Delta U = Q + W\). Energy transfers always result in some heat loss to the environment. |
| 3.8 | 30 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Describe the concept of [efficiency](heating-processes.md#efficiency). Solve problems using \(\eta = \frac{\text{energy output}}{\text{energy input}} \times 100\%\). Science as a Human Endeavour — steam engines to internal combustion engines. |

## Topic 2: Ionising radiation and nuclear reactions (15 hours)

### Module 4 — Nuclear model and stability

| Lesson | Time | Focus | Subject matter |
|--------|------|-------|---------------|
| 4.1 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Describe the [nuclear model of the atom](nuclear-physics.md#nuclear-model). Describe nuclides using \({}^A_Z X\) nomenclature. |
| 4.2 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Explain why protons in the nucleus repel each other. Describe the concept of the [strong nuclear force](nuclear-physics.md#strong-nuclear-force). Explain nuclear stability in terms of strong nuclear force, electrostatic repulsion, and relative number of protons and neutrons. |

### Module 5 — Radioactive decay and half-life

| Lesson | Time | Focus | Subject matter |
|--------|------|-------|---------------|
| 5.1 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Explain natural radioactive decay in terms of stability. Define alpha, beta positive, beta negative and gamma radiation. Describe penetrating ability, charge, mass and ionisation ability. |
| 5.2 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Explain how an excess of protons, neutrons or mass can result in alpha, beta positive and beta negative decay. Solve problems involving [balancing nuclear equations](nuclear-physics.md#nuclear-equations). |
| 5.3 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Explain how a radionuclide will, through a series of spontaneous decays, become a stable nuclide. Define [half-life](nuclear-physics.md#half-life). Solve radioactive decay problems involving whole numbers of half-lives. |
| 5.4 | 60 min | <span class="badge-practical">Practical</span> | **Practical: Modelling radioactive decay.** Use simulations (e.g. PhET Alpha Decay / Beta Decay) to investigate decay processes. |

### Module 6 — Nuclear energy and mass defect

| Lesson | Time | Focus | Subject matter |
|--------|------|-------|---------------|
| 6.1 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Describe fission and fusion reactions. Solve problems involving [mass defect](nuclear-physics.md#mass-defect) and binding energy using \(E = mc^2\). |
| 6.2 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Interpret a binding energy per nucleon curve. Explain why different elements release energy in fission vs fusion. SHE — nuclear energy applications. |
| 6.3 | 30 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | **Review:** Ionising radiation and nuclear reactions. |

## Topic 3: Electrical circuits (15 hours)

### Module 7 — Current, potential difference and energy flow

| Lesson | Time | Focus | Subject matter |
|--------|------|-------|---------------|
| 7.1 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Define [electric charge, conventional current and electron flow](electrical-circuits.md#electric-current). Solve problems involving \(I = \frac{q}{t}\). |
| 7.2 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Define [electrical potential difference](electrical-circuits.md#potential-difference) and EMF. Solve problems involving \(V = \frac{W}{q}\). |
| 7.3 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Describe the concept of [resistance](electrical-circuits.md#resistance). Solve problems using Ohm's law \(V = IR\). |
| 7.4 | 60 min | <span class="badge-practical">Practical</span> | **Practical: Ohmic and non-ohmic resistors.** Compare characteristics experimentally. Interpret graphical representations of V vs I data to find resistance using the gradient. |
| 7.5 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Describe the concept of [electrical power](electrical-circuits.md#power). Solve problems using \(P = VI\) and \(P = I^2R\). |

### Module 8 — Circuit analysis and design

| Lesson | Time | Focus | Subject matter |
|--------|------|-------|---------------|
| 8.1 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Describe series and parallel connections. Solve problems involving [equivalent resistance](electrical-circuits.md#series-and-parallel) in series and parallel circuits. |
| 8.2 | 60 min | <span class="badge-practical">Practical</span> | **Practical: Series and parallel circuits.** Investigate voltage and current distribution. |
| 8.3 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Solve problems involving [circuit analysis](electrical-circuits.md#circuit-analysis) — finding equivalent resistance, potential difference and current in series/parallel circuits. |
| 8.4 | 60 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | Describe power dissipation over resistors. Construct electrical circuit diagrams using standard symbols. Describe simple series, parallel and series/parallel circuits. |
| 8.5 | 60 min | <span class="badge-practical">Practical</span> | **Practical: Simple circuits for real-life purposes.** Investigate simple circuits for specific applications. |
| 8.6 | 30 min | <span class="badge-theory">Theory</span> | **Review:** Electrical circuits. |
